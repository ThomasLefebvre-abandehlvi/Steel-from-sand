Unit I: Zenith of Light

The Fall from Grace

Chapter 1: The Sky of Neo-Paris

Thomas Lefebvre looked out over the violet, curated horizon of Neo-Paris 2125. The Spire hummed with the sound of a trillion clean energy bytes, and the world was a masterpiece of frictionless living. He was the Lead Architect, the man who had perfected the art of building cities from nothing but light and graphene. To Thomas, the world was a solved equation, a series of materials waiting to be commanded. But perfection was a fragile thing, and far above the Spire, the sun was beginning to bleed.

Chapter 5: The Fall of Thomas

Thomas fell through the sky of a different world. He saw the shimmering towers of Neo-Paris dissolve into a grey, smog-choked horizon. When he finally hit the ground, the impact was cold, wet, and violent. He didn't land on a sterile glass floor; he landed in the mud. He looked at his hands—they were covered in black grease. He was in a scrapyard. The year was 2026, and the Lead Architect had just become the lowest material on Earth.

Unit II: Scrapyard Kingdom

The Detroit Mud

Chapter 6: The Detroit Mud

The rain felt like acid against his skin. Thomas stood in the middle of a graveyard of rusted steel, surrounded by the skeletons of cars that had been dead for fifty years. His 2125 Aero-Mesh suit was flickering, its power-cells fried by the flare. He tried to access his internal database, but all he found was a "Connection Error." For the first time in his life, Thomas Lefebvre was truly alone. He was just a man in a wet suit, standing in the ruins of a century that didn't know he existed.
